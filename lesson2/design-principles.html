Apple's webpage is very crisp and clean looking. Inbetween each item they
have a small amount of noticable white space. Each product lines up nicely
with the margins and things are simple and professional.

Visual Hierarchy

Simply Chocolate

simplychocolate.dk

Simply Chocolate (in Denmark) does a wonderful job at demonstrating Visual Hierarchy. Immediently when you go on their site, your attention is Immediently captivated by the moving pictures and texts across the screen. They have their photos listed out and published in a way that doesn't take away from the product, but rather, enhances it.

Hick's Law

America First Credit Union

americafirst.com

Ameriica First Credit Union is a prime example of Hick's Law. As we've all learned in our beginning code classes, "Keep It Simple Stupid". America First keeps their main menu (the three bars on the top right corner) small, and almost unnoticable. The tabs and links that they want you to click are listed just below in a bright and easily read font. They draw attention towards what they want you to pay attention to, and minimize (or simplify) everything they don't particularly care if you click on.
